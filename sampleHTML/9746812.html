{{Infobox NFLactive}}James Jones (born March 31, 1984 in San Jose, California) is an American football wide receiver. He was drafted by the Green Bay Packers in the 3rd round in the 2007 NFL Draft.

Career

High school

Jones was a decorated athlete at San Jose's Gunderson High School,{{Fact}} where he starred in basketball, track, and football. During his senior year, Jones was his teams most valuable player as a quarterback.{{Fact}}

College

After three nondescript years at San Jose State, Jones had a breakthrough 2006 senior season, where he caught 70 passes, good for 893 yards and 10 TDs. Jones capped off his senior season by catching 6 passes (106 yards), 2 for touchdowns, en route to being named the offensive MVP of the inaugural New Mexico Bowl. His accomplishments his senior year earned him 2nd team All-WAC honors along with an invite to the NFL Combine in Indianapolis, Indiana.

Professional

Jones was a starter for the Packers in the first game of the 2007 season. He caught 4 passes for 29 total yards in a 16-13 victory over the Philadelphia Eagles.

Jones caught his first touchdown during a 23-16 victory over the Minnesota Vikings. It was a 33 yard pass down the right sidelines. Jones finished the game with 4 catches for 49 yards.

Jones had his worst game of his young professional career against the Chicago Bears on October 7th, 2007. In this game Jones had two costly fumbles for the Packers as the Bears came back from a 10 point halftime deficit to beat the Packers 27-20.

Jones was announced as the Diet Pepsi 2007 Rookie of the Week for games played on October 28-29, 2007. Jones made three receptions for 107 yards including a first-quarter 79-yard touchdown in the Packers' 19 - 13 win over the Denver Broncos. Jones is currently the 3rd wide-receiver on the team, behind Donald Driver and Greg Jennings. [1]
