{{Football player infobox}} Darren Craddock (born February 23 1985 in Bishop Auckland, England) is an English footballer, currently playing for York City.

He started his career with Hartlepool United in 2004. He joined York City on a month's loan in 2006, who he joined permamently later that year, after making 24 league appearances for Hartlepool.

Career

Craddock was handed his first professional contract by Hartlepool United in June 2004.[1] He was signed by York City on a month's loan in January 2006.[2] He was released by Hartlepool United at the end of the 2005–06 season.[3]

He signed for York in May 2006, following his release by Hartlepool.[4] He was warned he could face a spell on the sidelines by manager Billy McEwan after receiving a one-match ban after he was booked for fouling Forest Green Rovers striker Allan Russell after losing possession on the halfway line.[5] He missed the rest of the 2006–07 season due to a fractured jaw, which was sustained against Cambridge United.[6] He was offered a new contract by York at the end of the 2006â€“07 season.[7]
