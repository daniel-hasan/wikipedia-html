Nikolai Vasilievich Ogarkov ({{lang-ru}}) (October 30, 1917 in the village of Molokovo, near Tver - January 23, 1994), was promoted to Marshal of the Soviet Union in 1977. Between 1977 and 1984 he was Chief of the General Staff of the USSR. He was fired by General Secretary Konstantin Chernenko in 1984 for his advocacy of less spending on consumer goods, and increased spending on weapons research and development.

He is generally credited with recognising that U.S superiority in information technology in the 1970's was leading to a "military technical revolution" (MTR). This wold be referred to in the United States as the Revolution in Military Afffairs.
