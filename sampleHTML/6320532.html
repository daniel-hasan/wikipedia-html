“I Take Thee Quagmire” is the twenty first episode of season five of Family Guy. Originally broadcast on 12 March, 2006,[1] guests on the episode included Adam West, Nicole Sullivan and Danny Smith.[2] The plot follows Peter being a contestant on Wheel of Fortune, and thus, winning himself a free maid for a week. However, upon seeing her, Quagmire instantly becomes attracted to her, this eventually leading him to propose marriage, to which she accepts. The couple are married, much to Peter's dismay; however, Quagmire begins to have seconds thoughts, but is shocked when Joan, his new wife, threatens to self-harm herself if he leaves, so he fakes his own death, with aid from Peter. Meanwhile, Stewie does not take well to Lois's decision of not to breastfeed him anymore, only for him to be reallowed to after conquering his addiction.[3] Several scenes were censored for television broadcasting. The episode received little reception, with one positive comment from IGN.

Plot

The show begins with Peter being a contestant of Wheel of Fortune, and thus winning. When he wins, he is permitted to choose a selection of prizes, one of which is one week's worth of free maid service, which he chooses. Meanwhile, Lois begins to question whether she should breastfeed Stewie any longer, as he is beginning to hurt her when feeding. Peter's free maid arrives, however, Peter fails to understand that the maid is a native speaker of the English language, and begins speaking in simplified terms with her. Joan, Peter's maid begins her work by pulling items out of Peter's belly, the moving on to carrying Peter on her back when he wants to go to the local store. Meanwhile, Lois decides to refrain from breast-feeding Stewie, after advice by Brian, who slowly responds after Lois shows him her breasts. Upon meeting her, Quagmire invites her out with him the following day, where he exclaims to himself after leaving Peter's house that he is in love.

Peter decides to give his maid an unnecessary amount of work for her final night, he does this by filling a watermelon full of fire crackers and giving it to Meg, only for it to explode all around her. Meanwhile, Stewie does not take kindly to not being breast fed. Several weeks later, Quagmire proposes marriage to Joan, to which she accepts and the couple begin to prepare their marriage ceremony, much to Cleveland, Joe and Peter's shock. Lois, Peter and their neghbours visit Quagmire's house, only to find that he has changed his personality and has a different outlook on life, however, Peter still believing this to be a prank, attempts to Quagmire by showing him pornographic magazines. Meanwhile, Stewie becomes increasingly stressed about not being breast-fed, thus resorting to jump from a playground swing on to another woman's breasts, only for Lois to have to pull him away, after he exclaims that the woman's breasts are lies. After discovering her bra size has dramatically increased, Lois's clothes begin to break, however, after Peter spills wine on her, he goes to shake her dry, thus making Quagmire think twice about his new marriage after seeing Lois's boobs shake. Meanwhile, Quagmire discusses with his friends how he can end his relationship, but upon telling Joan that he wants a divorce, she tells him that she would cut herself if he divorced her.

Peter decides that if Quagmire still wants to leave the relationship, he should fake his own death, so Peter resumes to show Joan a video of Quagmire being harmed by a ninja, then being attacked by Nazi's, then a 'pots and pans' robot, with the body being eaten by a dinosaur being held by Peter's hand. Unconvinced by the story, the guy's operate plan B which consists of Quagmire returning home and suffering from a heart attack. Meanwhile, Stewie beats his addiction to breast milk, only to be allowed to resume it when Lois realises that she is not ready to stop Stewie drinking her breast milk. Quagmire is buried in a coffin with oxygen which will last him thirt minutes, however, the funeral is interrupted by Peter when Mayor West informs everyone that all bodies have to be secured into the ground with concrete in order to rest his fear of zombies. However when Death shows up to retrieve Quagmire's body, he ends up taking his wife, Joan instead, as she was suicidal. en inform Death that her last name was Quagmire and that she was suicidal, and so Death takes her body, allowing Quagmire to live.[4]

Production

This episode is one of the first to begin with a scene which is not the Griffin family household or the town centre, but rather to begin by depicting the game show Wheel of Fortune.[5] When Brian is watching Malcolm in the Middle on television, a scene ensues showing the mother of the fictional family screaming at the rest of them, Jane Kaczmarek was asked to do the voice, however she refused; which MacFarlane comments that he gets the message that Kaczmarek wishes to portray that character as likeable, and does not wish to jeopardise that.[6] The father during that scene was voiced by Bryan Cranston, who accepted the role.[6] Seth MacFarlane comments that "this is the first real Quagmire story we've ever done."[6] A scene was made of Stewie attending a meeting similar to those at Alcoholics Anonymous, however this one was designed for people who are too addicted to breast milk.[7] The format of Wheel of Fortune is somewhat similar to the layout of the modern-day program, however the scene also features several things which are now no longer boradcast, and were featured in the 1980s.[6]

Censorship

Two scenes during this episode were censored for television broadcasting. When Quagmire first meets Joan and invites her out on a date, Cleveland states "is that a banana in your pocket, or an erection in your pocket"; although this entire scene was not censored, Fox Broadcasting Company bleeped "erection".[8] When Peter is promoting 'Crystal Pepsi', this scene was only shown on the DVD, as it is prohibited to promote one product over another on television.[6]

Cultural references

When Mayor West is playing as a contestant on Jeopardy!, he spells the name of the host, Alex Trebek backwards, thus sending him back to the fifth dimension, a reference to former animated comedy series: Super Friends.[6] When Peter and the children are flying through space and singing, this references The Great Space Coaster.[6] The two Asian men who meet Peter at the traffic lights when riding Joan's back speak in a very similar tone to Howard Cossell, a reference to 1985 cult film Better Off Dead.[7][6] When Quagmire fantasises about being alone in the forest with Joan and speaking to her in Elvish, this references a scene from Lord of the Rings.[6][8]

Reception

This episode received little reception, however IGN's Michael Drucker commented that "the Volume four episodes are easily funnier than the volume three ones", this referring to the episode.[9]
