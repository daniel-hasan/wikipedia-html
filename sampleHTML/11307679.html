Doris Packer, real name Doris Edwards, (May 30 1904 â€” March 31 1979) was an American actress best known for her role as Mrs. Rayburn, Theodore Cleaver's principal in Leave It to Beaver, although she is also remembered for her role as the mother of Chatsworth Osborne, Jr. in The Many Loves of Dobie Gillis.

Packer was married to renowned stage director Rowland G. Edwards for twenty-five years, from 1928 until his death in 1953. Packer outlived her husband by twenty-six years, and died from natural causes in Glendale, California, on March 31, 1979, at the age of 74.
