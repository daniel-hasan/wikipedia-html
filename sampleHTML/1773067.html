The 2005 National Football League Draft , took place on April 23 and April 24 2005[1] at the Jacob K. Javits Convention Center in New York City, New York. The draft is the 70th draft in NFL history.[2] The NFL Draft had been held at Madison Square Garden since 1995, but was moved to the Javits Center in 2005.[3] The draft featured the San Francisco 49ers selecting first overall Alex Smith from the University of Utah. Mr. Irrelevant, the final pick of the draft, Andy Stokes from William Penn University was selected by the New England Patriots. Thirty-two compensatory choices were distributed amongst fourteen teams, with the Philadelphia Eagles and the St. Louis Rams garnering the most with 4 picks each.[4] Three of the first five picks were running backs, an NFL Draft first.[5] The 255 players chosen in the draft were composed of:[6] {{TOCleft}}

  • 50 defensive backs
  • 36 linebackers
  • 30 wide receivers
  • 26 offensive tackles
  • 26 running backs
  • 20 defensive ends
  • 15 defensive tackles
  • 15 guards
  • 14 quarterbacks
  • 9 tight ends
  • 8 centers
  • 3 kickers
  • 2 punters
  • 1 kick returner
