William Leigh Brent (1784-1848) was a U.S. Representative representing Louisiana's 3rd congressional district. Brent was born at Port Tobacco, Maryland, on February 20, 1784 the nephew of Virginia congressman Richard Brent. He studied law and was admitted to the bar of Maryland and moved to Louisiana about 1809 and commenced the practice of law there. Brent was appointed by President Madison as Deputy Attorney General for the western district of the Territory of Orleans. In 1822 he was elected as an Adams-Clay Republican to the Eighteenth Congress and as an Adams Republican to the Nineteenth, and Twentieth Congresses. He served from March 4, 1823-March 3, 1829. Rep. Brent was a founding member of Louisiana's Whig Party, but was not again a candidate for public office.
