Ailill Inbanda mac Eógain(d.550)[1] was a king of Connacht from the Ui Fiachrach branch of the Connachta. He was the son and sucessor of Eógan Bél[2] who was slain by the northern Ui Neill in 543/547. His nickname Inbanda means "womanish" or effeminate.

His father's feud with the northern Ui Neill continued. He himself was slain at the Battle of Cúil Conaire in Cera(Mayo Co.) along with his brother Áed Fortobol ("the strong") by Fergus and Domnall of the Cenél nEógain, sons of Muirchertach mac Ercae.
