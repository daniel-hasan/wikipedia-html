{{Infobox Television episode}} “Stewie Loves Lois” is the first episode of season five of Family Guy. The episode first aired on September 10, 2006.[1] Guest stars on the show were Alec Sulkin and Danny Smith.[2] The plot follows Stewie's beloved teddy bear, Rupert, almost being destroyed by a dog. However, after Lois fixes the teddy, he begins to like her. Meanwhile, Peter sues his local doctor for raping him, despite the fact he only gave him a standard prostate exam — only to realize it was a standard procedure at a later time.[3] The episode received both positive and negative comments from IGN.

Plot

Quahog becomes the subject of a flu epidemic, and Peter goes to see Dr. Hartman for a vaccine. Although they are in short supply, Peter manages to get receive anyway (by falling on to the syringe). While looking through Peter's papers, Hartman realizes that Peter needs a prostate exam. Peter agrees to get one, despite not knowing what it is. Peter is shocked by the concept of the exam, especially after Hartman puts his finger up his anus. Upon running home to tell Lois about his ordeal, she is non-supportive, much to his disappointment. He then resorts to informing his friends about the incident, to which they tell him Hartman "raped" them too. As a result of this, Peter decides to sue Hartman in a court of law.

Meanwhile, Stewie plays at the park with his teddy, Rupert. However, his teddy bear is almost destroyed by a dog. Lois retrieves then fixes the bear. Stewie then becomes dependent on Lois, which she takes as refreshing at first, but eventually, she becomes exhausted at Stewie to the extent of her having a nightmare of harming him. Despite attempts by Lois not to sue the doctor for a standard exam, Peter ignores her, taking him to court. While there, Peter unintentionally exaggerates the story, however, the judge is not convinced, until Peter tells him to remember his prostate exam by Hartman, to which he immediately exclaims: "Guilty, guilty, guilty". Because of this, Hartman's medical license is revoked. Peter celebrates his victory at The Drunken Clam, however begins to go to the bathroom frequently, causing concern among his friends. Peter is then informed that his prostate may be infected, and that he is in need of a prostate examination. However, his friends inform him that the chances of a doctor examining him after the Hartman incident are slim. Worried by this, he attempts to trick Hartman into performing the exam, however this fails — but Hartman agrees to perform the exam anyway.[4]

Production

When Stewie is fantasizing about murdering and harming Lois, he performs several karate and kung-fu moves. This is a reference to an individual who knew Mark Hentemann, a writer for Family Guy used to know. He was the boy who would come to school, car washes or other events and tell stories about the people who he had been in physical fights with.[5] During the scene when Stewie describes Lois as being the female version of Bonnie Hunt, MacFarlane comments that he feels bad about that joke, as just before it was broadcast — Hunt telephoned him on his mobile phone and told him about how much she enjoyed the show, however the gag was not removed due to its expected airing date being so close to when the telephone call was made.[5]

For a reason which was not specified, the sound of Lois's footsteps when going down the basement stairs during her dream of killing Stewie, had to be overcome by Stewie talking more often. This line of Stewie making general conversation in the same scene, was altered several times due to lack of laughter from the audience.[5] MacFarlane comments that the scene of Peter standing up to his teacher who was giving the class a written examination, is a reference to "80's TV bullshit".[5][6]

Reception

IGNs Dan Iverson commented that "the Stewie/Lois story was really kind of funny", noting that the scene was "a nice window in on parenting". He describes the scene of Stewie shouting at Brian in Spanish as being "very funny". However, Iverson criticized the episode, noting that the scene of Peter giving his story in court went from just "creepy and awkward to just plain disturbing". The episode received a final rating by Iverson of 4/5.[7]

Cultural references

During the scene when a car is about to fall off a cliff, Robin the Boy Wonder appears and attempts to save the people. However, upon arrival he is criticized as he will not be able to save them, this is a reference to the portrayal of Robin from the 1960s Batman series. Throughout the episode, other references are made to fictitious super heroes. In total, three references were made to Star Trek. When Peter is memorising what Hartman did to him, He goes into a flashback in court, and everything turns black and white and grainy, this is a reference to "lifetime movie" The Accused.[5][6]

Lois makes a reference to Barbara Bush after waking up from her dream of killing Stewie. She claims "I'm just like that Texas woman who held her son underwater and give him brain damage, I'm just like Barbara Bush". When the fisherman is telling Peter about the serious consequences of not getting a prostate exam, he shows Peter a picture of band Primus, to which Peter asks him why he carries such random pictures around with him[8]
