'''Ganmain''' is a town in the [[Riverina]] region of [[New South Wales]], [[Australia]].  Ganmain is located around 55 kilometres north west of [[Wagga Wagga, New South Wales|Wagga Wagga]], and 50 kilomtres east of [[Narrandera, New South Wales|Narrandera]].  Ganmain is in the [[Coolamon Shire Council|Coolamon Shire]] [[Local Government Areas in Australia|Local Government Area]] and had a population at the 2006 census of 648.<ref name="ABS">{{Census 2006 AUS

The town name is said to be an [[Indigenous Australian|Aboriginal]] word meaning "Crown scenes on the Moon for tribal reasons" or "native decorated with stars"<ref>[http://www.gnb.nsw.gov.au/name/extract?id=JPwGXtWAMa New South Wales Geographical Names Board]. - Retrieved [[11 January]] [[2007]].</ref>

Ganmain is the self proclaimed "Sheaf Hay centre of Australia" and has produced [[chaff]] for many leading racehorse trainers in Australia.  The Big Haystack in Pioneer Park reflects this background.

The town has two primary schools, Ganmain Public School and St. Brendan's Catholic Primary School.

[[Australian rules football]] is the most popular sport with the local team, Ganmain-[[Grong Grong, New South Wales|Grong Grong]]-[[Matong, New South Wales|Matong]] playing in the [[Riverina Football League]].
