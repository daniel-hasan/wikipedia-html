The '''Synod of Twyford'''  was a synod of the early English church held in 684 and described by [[Bede]] in his [[Historia ecclesiastica gentis Anglorum]], Book IV, ch. 28.  The synod was held at a place called "Adtwifyrdi", the location of which is unknown. [[Theodore of Tarsus|Archbishop Theodore]] presided over the synod in the presence of King Ecgfrith; bishop [[Trumbert|Tunberht]] of [[Hexham]] was deposed there and [[Cuthbert of Lindisfarne|Cuthbert]] elected [[bishop of Lindisfarne]].<ref>Bede, IV.28</ref>
