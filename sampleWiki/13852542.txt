== Background ==
According to Pfitzner's estranged father, she had three children, each with a different father. Dean had an older half-sister and a younger half-brother. <ref name="Charged"> </ref> <ref name="Too Late"> </ref>

== Government involvement ==
The New South Wales Department of [[List of New South Wales government agencies#Community Services|Community Services]] (DOCS) had sought a [[Family court]] warrant on [[11 October]] to place Dean in the custody of his grandmother. When the order was served on Pfitzner on [[18 October]] she advised police that Dean was already in the custody of DOCS. When it was subsequently realised that this was not correct police questioned Pfitzner further, leading to her arrest. <ref name="Too Late"> </ref> Both DOCS and the New South Wales [[Ombudsman]] are conducting inquiries into the involvement of DOCS. <ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/news/national/minister-avoids-queries-on-dead-boy/2007/10/22/1192940960175.html|title=Minister ducks queries on dead boy|publisher=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=[[2007-10-22]]|accessdate = 2007-10-22}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |first= |last= |authorlink= |coauthors= |title= Where the good times roll children are dying|url= http://www.nzherald.co.nz/section/2/story.cfm?c_id=2&objectid=10477378&pnum=0|work= |publisher= New Zealand Herald |date= 21 November 2007 |accessdate= 2007-12-15}} </ref>

== Community reaction ==
Many local residents gathered in Mandurama Reserve in the days following the discovery. An impromptu shrine of flowers and toys developed near the waters edge. 

When police escorted Pfitzner to the scene on 20 October the crowd jeered and surged towards the car. <ref name="Too Late"> </ref>

A community ceremony for Dean held on 26 October at Mandurama Reserve and attended by 2000 people included prayers, balloons and floating candles. <ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/news/national/candles-and-balloons-for-lake-angel/2007/10/23/1192941046738.html|title=Candles and balloons for 'Lake Angel'|publisher=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=[[2007-10-23]]|accessdate = 2007-10-23}}</ref> <ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/news/National/Mourners-pay-respects-to-Shillingsworth/2007/10/26/1192941328675.html/2007/10/23/1192941046738.html|title=Mourners pay respects to Shillingsworth'|publisher=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=[[2007-10-26]]|accessdate = 2007-10-26}}</ref> As an [[Indigenous Australians|Aboriginal]] Australian, Dean was accorded a [[smoking ceremony]].

== Funeral ==
Dean Shillingsworth's funeral was conducted in [[Brewarrina, New South Wales|Brewarrina]] his hometown. It was attended by over 300 family, friends and community members. It was held on Thursday, [[1 November]]. <ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.theaustralian.news.com.au/story/0,25197,22685666-26103,00.html|title=Hundreds attend Shillingsworth funeral|publisher=[[The Australian]]|date=[[2007-11-01]]|accessdate = 2007-12-13}}</ref>

== Court case ==
Rachel Pfitzner appeared in court on [[12 December]] [[2007]] via videolink from [[Silverwater Women's Correctional Centre]]. The case was adjourned without bail until [[6 February]] [[2008]]. <ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/news/national/compassion-plea-as-mother-of-boy-in-suitcase-faces-court/2007/12/12/1197135558270.html|title=Compassion plea as mother of boy in suitcase faces court|publisher=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|date=[[2007-12-13]]|accessdate = 2007-12-13}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |first= AAP|last= |authorlink= |coauthors= |title= Murder-accused mum 'deserves compassion'|url= http://www.thewest.com.au/aapstory.aspx?StoryName=444269 |work= |publisher= The West Australian|date= 12 December 2007|accessdate= 2007-12-15}} </ref>
