The ([[Roman Catholic]]) '''[[Diocese]] of [[Ubon Ratchathani]]''' (''Dioecesis Ubonratchathaniensis'', {{lang-th|สังฆมณฑลอุบลราชธานี}}) is located in the north-east of [[Thailand]]. It is a suffragan diocese of the [[archdiocese of Thare and Nonseng]].

The diocese covers an area of 53,917 [[square kilometer|km²]], covering seven [[provinces of Thailand]] - [[Amnat Charoen Province|Amnat Charoen]], [[Maha Sarakham Province|Maha Sarakham]], [[Roi Et Province|Roi Et]], [[Sisaket Province|Sisaket]], [[Surin Province|Surin]], [[Ubon Ratchathani Province|Ubon Ratchathani]] and [[Yasothon Province|Yasothon]]. As of 2001, of the 7.7 million citizen 24,760 are member of the Catholic Church. It is divided into 55 parishes, having 42 priests altogether.

==History==
The [[Vicariate Apostolic]] of Ubon was created on  [[May 7]] [[1953]], when the [[archdiocese of Thare and Nonseng|Vicariate Apostolic of Thare]] was split. In 1965 the western part of the territory was split off and became the [[Diocese of Nakhon Ratchasima|Vicariate Apostolic of Nakhon Ratchasima]]. On [[December 18]] [[1965]] the Vicariate Apostolic was elevated to a diocese.

==Cathedral==
The [[cathedral]] of the diocese is the ''Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception''<!--อาสนวิหารแม่พระนิรมล-->, located in Ubon Ratchathani.
