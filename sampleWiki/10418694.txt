[[York City F.C.]] is an [[England|English]] [[football (soccer)|football]] club based in [[York]], [[North Yorkshire]]. The club was originally formed in 1908, and played several seasons in the [[Midland Football League|Midland League]], before closing during the [[First World War]]. The club was reformed in 1922, and was elected to play in the Midland League. The team played at this level until 1929, when they were elected  to [[the Football League]]. They played in [[Football League Third Division North|Division Three North]] from their year of entry until the [[1958-59 in English football|1958&ndash;59]] season, when the League was re-organised and they were placed in [[Football League Fourth Division|Division Four]]. During this period, the team reached the semi-final of [[the FA Cup]], being beaten by [[Football League First Division|Division One]] side [[Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United]] in the replay.

The team won its first [[playoff|play-off]] in 1993, when they beat [[Crewe Alexandra F.C.|Crewe Alexandra]] on penalties in the final at [[Wembley Stadium (1923)|Wembley Stadium]]. The following season saw another play-off appearance. York were beaten by [[Stockport County F.C.|Stockport County]] in the semi-final. The club was relegated to the [[Football Conference]] in 2004, ending 75 years of League membership. York remain in this division, but made it to the play-offs in the [[2006-07 in English football|2006&ndash;07]] season, where they lost to [[Morecambe F.C.|Morecambe]] in the semi-final.

==1922&ndash;1938==
[[Image:YCFC1922.jpg|right|thumb|250px|York City squad of 1922.]]
At a meeting held at the Co-operative Hall in York on [[6 May]], [[1922]], the decision was taken to form the '''York City Football and Athletic Club Limited'''. The club attempted to gain admission to [[the Football League]],<ref name="Complete record 12">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p12 }}</ref> but were elected instead to play in the [[Midland Football League|Midland League]].<ref name="Complete record 13">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p13 }}</ref> York's first game was against [[Notts County F.C.|Notts County]] Reserves, an away match played in September 1922, which, despite a good performance, York lost 4&ndash;2.<ref name="Complete record 15">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p15 }}</ref> As their [[Fulfordgate]] ground was not ready, York had to play their first two home matches at Mille Crux ground  of Messrs Rowntree & Company Limited.<ref name="Complete record 15"/> York's first game at [[Fulfordgate]], which finished as a 4&ndash;1 victory against [[Mansfield Town F.C.|Mansfield Town]] eventually came on [[20 September]], [[1922]].<ref name="Complete record 15"/> York finished the [[1922-23 in English football|1922&ndash;23]] season in 19th place out of 22 in the Midland League. They had been in a mid-table position in early March 1923, but failed to win any of their remaining 14 fixtures.<ref name="Complete record 15"/> In the same year  the team reached the final of the North Riding Senior Cup, but lost 4&ndash;2 to [[Middlesbrough F.C.|Middlesbrough]] Reserves on [[10 March]], [[1923]].<ref name="Complete record 15"/><ref name="YORK CITY">{{cite web
| title = YORK CITY
| url = http://www.fchd.info/YORKC.HTM

For the [[1924-25 in English football|1924&ndash;25]] season, the Midland League was reorganised because eight reserve Football League teams withdrew from the competition.  | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p16 }}</ref> The following season, the Midland League was restored to its previous size, and York finished in 16th position, after suffering a bad start to the season from which the team never recovered.<ref name="Complete record 17">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p17 }}</ref> The club had their most successful season in the Midland League in [[1926-27 in English football|1926&ndash;27]], when they finished in 6th place out of 20 and reached the second round of the FA Cup, losing 2&ndash;1 to [[Grimsby Town F.C.|Grimsby Town]]. This was the first time York had passed the competition's qualifying rounds.<ref name="YORK CITY"/><ref name="Complete record 18">{{cite book
 | last = Batters

 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p411 }}</ref> The following  season, the club made its first serious attempt for election into the Football League, which, despite gaining much encouragement from outside sources, was unsuccessful, with [[Barrow A.F.C.|Barrow]] and [[Accrington Stanley F.C.|Accrington Stanley]] being re-elected instead.<ref name="Complete record 18"/> Following their failure to gain election, the side planned ahead, which included the appointment of [[Jock Collier]] as player-manager in July 1928.<ref name="Complete record 21">{{cite book

In the [[1976-77 in English football|1976&ndash;77]] season  York faced a second successive relegation, by dropping into Division Four after finishing bottom of Division Three.<ref name="Final 1976/1977 English Division 3 (old) Table">{{cite web
| title = Final 1976/1977 English Division 3 (old) Table

| publisher = Soccerbase
| accessdate = 2007-11-17}}</ref> Manager Wilf McGuinness was dismissed in October 1977, with [[Charlie Wright]] being named his successor in November.<ref name="Complete record 87">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p87 }}</ref> The [[1977-78 in English football|1977&ndash;78]] season finished with York 22nd in Division Four and they were forced to apply for re-election, whilst the club was in financial trouble and attendances were at an all-time low.<ref name="Brief History"/><ref name="Complete record 87"/><ref name="Final 1977/1978 English Division 4 Table">{{cite web
| title = Final 1977/1978 English Division 4 Table
| url = http://www.soccerbase.com/league2.sd?competitionid=7&seasonid=107&teamid=2910
| publisher = Soccerbase
| accessdate = 2007-11-17}}</ref> Charlie Wright's contact as manager was terminated in March 1980 and [[Barry Lyons]] was named as caretaker manager until the end of the [[1979-80 in English football|1979&ndash;80]] season.<ref name="Complete record 90">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p90 }}</ref> After finishing the season in 17th position in the table, Lyons was appointed manager on a full-time basis.<ref name="Complete record 91">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p91 }}</ref>

==1980&ndash;1999==
York finished bottom of Division Four in [[1980-81 in English football|1980&ndash;81]] and were forced once again to seek re-election.<ref name="Complete record 92">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p92 }}</ref> Lyons was sacked as manager in December 1981, with [[Kevin Randall]] being placed as caretaker manager, but he lasted only until March 1982 and was replaced by [[Barry Swallow]] for a further two months.<ref name="Complete record 93">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p93 }}</ref> The club made the appointments of [[Denis Smith (footballer)|Denis Smith]], who had played for the club on loan the previous season, as player-manager and and [[Viv Busby]] as player-coach in May 1982.<ref name="Complete record 93"/><ref name="Complete record 94">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p94 }}</ref> Under their guidance York won their first major honour, Division Four, in the [[1983-84 in English football|1983&ndash;84]] season, with a club record 101 points, becoming the first team to reach 100 points in the Football League.<ref name="Complete record 96">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p96 }}</ref><ref name="Football League points records">{{cite web
| title = Football League points records
| url = http://www.football-league.premiumtv.co.uk/page/RecordPoints/0,,10794,00.html
| publisher = The Football League
| accessdate = 2007-11-17}}</ref>

In January 1985, York beat Division One team [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] 1&ndash;0 in the fourth round of the FA Cup at Bootham Crescent, with the goal coming from a [[Keith Houchen]] penalty.<ref name="York 1 Arsenal 0">{{cite web
| title = York 1 Arsenal 0
| url = http://www.yorkcityfc.com/history/arsenal.php
| publisher = Red and Blue NET
| accessdate = 2007-03-31}}</ref> York went on to draw 1&ndash;1 with Liverpool at Bootham Crescent in February, but lost 7&ndash;0 in the replay at [[Anfield]] four days later in what was the club's record cup defeat.<ref name="Brief History"/><ref name="Complete record 99-100">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = pp99&ndash;100 }}</ref> The following season, York again played Liverpool in the FA Cup, being beaten in another replay 3&ndash;1 at Anfield.<ref name="Citizens and minstermen 3">{{cite book
 | last = Jarred
 | coauthors = & Windross
 | title = Citizens and Minstermen, A Who's Who of York City FC 1922-1997
 | pages = p3 }}</ref> The club's managerial team of Denis Smith and Viv Busby departed in May 1987 to take over at [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]], and [[Bobby Saxton]] was appointed as the new manager in June.<ref name="Complete record 105-107">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = pp105&ndash;107 }}</ref> Saxton's first season in charge ended in relegation, after the side finished 23rd in Division Three. This was, statistically, York's worst season in the Football League.<ref name="Final 1987/1988 English Division 3 (old) Table">{{cite web
| title = Final 1987/1988 English Division 3 (old) Table
| url = http://www.soccerbase.com/league2.sd?competitionid=7&seasonid=117&teamid=2910

| accessdate = 2007-11-18}}</ref><ref name="Complete record 107">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p107 }}</ref> Saxton resigned as manager in September 1988 and Barry Swallow again took charge on a caretaker basis,<ref name="Complete record 110">{{cite book
 | last = Batters
 | title = York City: A Complete Record 1922-1990
 | pages = p110 }}</ref> before [[John Bird (footballer)|John Bird]], formerly [[Hartlepool United F.C.|Hartlepool United]] manager, took charge in October and saw the team finish 11th in the table.<ref name="Complete record 110"/><ref name="Final 1988/1989 English Division 4 Table">{{cite web
| title = Final 1988/1989 English Division 4 Table
| url = http://www.soccerbase.com/league2.sd?competitionid=7&seasonid=118&teamid=2910
| publisher = Soccerbase
| accessdate = 2007-11-18}}</ref>
[[Image:YCFC1993.jpg|right|thumb|York players celebrate after beating Crewe in the play-off final.]]
In September 1990, [[David Longhurst]], a York player, died during a home match against [[Lincoln City F.C.|Lincoln City]] after suffering a heart attack on the field, and later the club named a stand at Bootham Crescent after him.<ref name="David Longhurst Stand">{{cite web

York recorded a victory over [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] in the [[Football League Cup|League Cup]] second round in September 1995, which saw United beaten 3&ndash;0 at [[Old Trafford (football ground)|Old Trafford]], in a season that United later went on to win the [[Premier League]] and [[FA Cup]] double.<ref name="When City rocked the world">{{cite web
