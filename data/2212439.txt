This page lists all the '''winners of the [[Eurovision Song Contest]]''', an annual competition organised by member countries of the [[European Broadcasting Union]]. The Contest, which has been broadcast every year since its debut in 1956, is one of the longest-running television programmes in the world. The Contest's winner has been determined using numerous [[Voting at the Eurovision Song Contest|voting techniques]] throughout its history; centre to these have been the awarding of points to countries by juries or [[televoting|televoters]]. The country awarded the most points is declared the winner.</onlyinclude><ref>[http://www.eurovision.tv/addons/files/Extract_ESC2007Rules.pdf Extract from the rules for the 2007 Eurovision Song Contest]. Eurovision.tv. Retrieved on [[22 August]] [[2007]].</ref><onlyinclude>

The Contest has produced fifty-five winners in fifty-two Contests: one every year except the tied [[Eurovision Song Contest 1969|1969 Contest]], which had four winners. Twenty-four different countries have won the Contest; the most recent addition to this list is [[Serbia in the Eurovision Song Contest|Serbia]], which won the Contest on its debut appearance in [[Eurovision Song Contest 2007|2007]].</onlyinclude><ref>Serbia were participating for the first time as an independent nation, previously they had competed as part of [[Serbia and Montenegro]] and [[Yugoslavia]]. [[Switzerland in the Eurovision Song Contest|Switzerland]] also won the Contest on their debut—the first Contest, in 1956.</ref><onlyinclude> The country with the highest number of wins is [[Ireland in the Eurovision Song Contest|Ireland]], with seven. [[Portugal in the Eurovision Song Contest|Portugal]] is the country with the longest history in the Contest without a win; it made its fortieth appearance at the 2007 Contest. The only person to have won more than once is [[Johnny Logan (singer)|Johnny Logan]], who won the Contest three times for  Ireland; once as a singer, in 1980; once as a singer-songwriter, in 1987; and once as a songwriter for [[Linda Martin]], in 1992.

Eleven Eurovision winners featured at the ''[[Congratulations (Eurovision)|Congratulations]]'' concert in 2005, in which [[ABBA]]'s "[[Waterloo (song)|Waterloo]]" was voted the most popular song of the Contest's first fifty years.<ref name="escwaterloo">[http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/4366574.stm ABBA win 'Eurovision 50th' vote]. BBC News ([[23 October]] [[2005]]). Retrieved on [[22 August]][[2007]].</ref> The most successful countries never to have won the Contest are Malta and Russia, having both finished second twice and third twice. The highest number of points scored by a winning country at Eurovision was the 292 points achieved by [[Finland]]'s [[Lordi]] at the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2006|2006 Contest]]. The lowest total is the eighteen points scored by the four winning countries in [[Eurovision Song Contest 1969|1969]]. The largest margin of victory was that of [[Katrina and The Waves]] in [[Eurovision Song Contest 1997|1997]], who won by 70 points.

The [[United Kingdom in the Eurovision Song Contest|United Kingdom]] has finished second fifteen times at Eurovision, more than any other country. Since the introduction of the current voting system in 1975, the winner of the Contest has been decided by the final voting nation on eleven occasions.<ref>1979, 1980, 1981, 1984, 1988, 1991, 1992, 1993, 1998, 2002 and 2003.</ref>
